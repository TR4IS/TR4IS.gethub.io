01 Overview

What This Is

Reachflow is a private network of handpicked developers. Each member gets a public-facing space hosted under their GitHub identity.

By creating or editing your space, you agree to everything on this page. If you disagree with any part — stop using your space.

02 Responsibility

Your Space, Your Problem

You own what you publish. We host it. Your space is publicly accessible on the internet, linked to your GitHub account, and entirely your responsibility.

If your content causes legal, ethical, or community problems — you are accountable, not reachflow.

03 Rules

What's Not Allowed

The following will get your space removed immediately, no warning.

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) — zero tolerance. Reported to authorities and GitHub.
  • Illegal content of any kind — piracy, fraud, hacking tools targeting others.
  • Hate speech, threats, or targeted harassment of any individual or group.
  • Spam, phishing, or malware distribution.
  • Content that infringes copyright, trademarks, or intellectual property.
  • Impersonation of any person or organization.
  • Anything designed to deceive, manipulate, or harm others.
04 Enforcement

How We Handle It

The site owner reserves the right to remove any space, at any time, with or without notice, for any violation of these terms.

  • Reachflow is curated — membership is a privilege, not a right.
  • Serious violations may be reported to GitHub and relevant authorities.
  • There is no formal appeals process. Owner discretion is final.
05 Privacy

What We Store

Reachflow stores only what GitHub provides at sign-in: your username, display name, bio, and avatar.

  • No passwords. No emails. No tracking cookies.
  • Your space content lives in a public GitHub repository.
  • Anyone can view or fork that repository — it's open source.

Updates

These terms can change at any time. Continued use of your space means you accept whatever they say at that point.
